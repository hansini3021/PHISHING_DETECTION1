
You are the wound
I never meant to make,
The silence that followed
Every careless word I said.

If regret had a heartbeat,
It would sound like your name
Echoing in rooms
Where you no longer stand.

I hurt you —
Not loudly,
Not in one single storm,
But in quiet moments
Where I should have chosen gentleness.

And now I miss you
In the smallest things —
Songs I skip,
Places I avoid,
Conversations that feel unfinished.

The cruelest part?
I cannot stay —
Because loving me
Feels like standing too close to fire.

And I cannot leave —
Because walking away
Feels like leaving my own breath behind.

So I live in the middle,
Between apology and pride,
Between reaching out
And letting you heal without me.

If you ever wonder —
You were never easy to lose.
You were the home
I didn’t know how to protect.
